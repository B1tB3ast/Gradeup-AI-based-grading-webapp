DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, is a molecule found in the nucleus of cells that carries genetic information. It functions as a code, with four different nitrogenous bases (A, T, C, G) forming sequences to encode instructions for the synthesis of proteins. Through processes like transcription and translation, DNA plays a critical role in the production of proteins, which are essential for various cellular functions and the overall development and functioning of living organisms.
