DNA is a molecule found in cells, and it carries genetic information. It has a double helix structure and consists of nucleotides with a phosphate group, a sugar molecule, and nitrogenous bases. DNA is involved in protein synthesis through transcription and translation. However, it doesn't directly produce energy; that's the role of other cellular processes like cellular respiration. It also doesn't determine eye color but contributes to the genetic information that influences it.
